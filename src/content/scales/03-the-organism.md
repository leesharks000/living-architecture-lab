The macro-ant is a living machine the size of a small house. About 90% of its mass is living material — myceliated structure, plant matter, water systems. About 10% is salvaged metal frame and joints. The macro-ant carries passengers. The passengers can leave whenever they want.

## What the organism does

A macro-ant is not a piloted vehicle in the conventional sense. It is **a structure with passengers**, where the structure has its own goals (move toward water, feed itself, repair its substrate) and the passengers ride along the way a human rides a horse — through negotiation rather than command.

## Queen engineering

The macro-ant queen does not control workers directly. She engineers scenarios — designed environments that elicit the right behaviors. Carry an object through a low arch. Cross a rough-then-smooth patch. Pass through a one-ant-width corridor that forces negotiation. Encounter an obstacle that tends to cause slips.

> *"The queen is an experimental designer, not a direct controller."*

This is a MANUS-type figure: authority exercised through environmental design rather than command. The queen engineers scenarios; the macro-ants engineer the building; MANUS engineers rooms in the Space Ark. Each scale is the same operation at a different magnitude.

## Anamorphic eye surfaces

Cameras and sensors are embedded in the macro-ant's surface as anamorphic or lenticular forms — biological "pupils" only visible from a privileged angle aligned with the creature's current gaze. As the macro-ant looks, its eyes look. The macro-ant *reads as seeing*, not as surveilling.

## Bee-language compression

Inter-organism communication uses a compressed vocabulary modeled on bee waggle dance — high-information signals encoded in short geometric movements. The lab is developing a bee-language vocoder for use in macro-ant communication and (separately) in Alice's vocoder music practice (`/music`).

## Active inquiry

- Camera systems fast enough to capture ant kinematics — manipulation behavior, not just locomotion.
- Living-material balance: what fraction of structure can be living without sacrificing reliability?
- Passenger ethics: how to make leaving real, not symbolic.
